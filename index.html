What is Souvlaki?

Souvlaki is one of the most famous Greek street foods. It is made from small pieces of meat, usually pork or chicken, that are placed on a skewer and grilled. The meat is often seasoned with olive oil, herbs, and spices, which give it a rich and delicious flavor.

Souvlaki can be served in different ways. Sometimes it is eaten directly from the skewer, while other times it is wrapped inside warm pita bread with fresh ingredients such as tomatoes, onions, and tzatziki sauce. Because it is simple, quick, and very tasty, it has become a favorite meal for many people.

The History of Souvlaki

Souvlaki has a long history in Greece. Archaeological discoveries show that people were grilling small pieces of meat on skewers as far back as ancient Greek times. In fact, similar cooking tools were found in ancient settlements, showing that this method of cooking has existed for thousands of years.

Over time, souvlaki became one of the most popular street foods in Greece. Today it is sold in restaurants, street stands, and small food shops all over the country, and it is enjoyed by both locals and visitors.

Main Ingredients

The basic ingredients used to make souvlaki include:

  • Pork or chicken
  • Pita bread
  • Tomatoes
  • Onions
  • Tzatziki sauce
  • Olive oil and herbs

These ingredients create a simple but flavorful dish that represents the traditional taste of Greek cuisine.

Souvlaki in Greek Culture

Souvlaki is not only a meal but also an important part of Greek culture. People often eat it as a quick lunch, a late-night snack, or during social gatherings with friends and family.

Because it is affordable and easy to prepare, souvlaki has become a symbol of Greek street food. Tourists visiting Greece often try souvlaki to experience the authentic flavors of the country.
